### Active Inspection — Paragraph Indentation Consistency

#### Motivation
Wrap environments in LaTeX can corrupt TeX's `\parindent` state if the everypar mechanism or `\noindent` insertion is not properly scoped. This analysis checks whether swarmwrap preserves correct paragraph indentation after figure environments end.

#### Method
Created `scripts/detect-indentation-issues.py` (v2). The script:
1. Extracts text lines with bounding boxes using PyMuPDF `get_text("dict")`
2. Classifies lines as full-width, narrow (wrapped), figure-zone, or header
3. Computes indent distribution per category
4. Flags anomalous high-indent full-width lines
5. Checks for missing indent on narrow-to-fullwidth transitions

#### Results

**Indent Distribution (all 3 PDFs):**
- All body text (narrow + full-width) has indent=45.8pt (the document's `\parindent`)
- stress-50: 128 full-width lines, 531 narrow lines. 96.1% of full-width at 45.8pt.
- customwrap: 107 full-width lines, 169 narrow lines. 57.9% at 45.8pt (rest are list items with different indent levels — expected)
- pbv: 168 full-width lines, 154 narrow lines. 85.1% at 45.8pt.

**Anomalous High-Indent Lines:**
- 5 in stress-50, 30 in customwrap, 9 in pbv flagged as "anomalous high indent (>100pt) on full-width lines"
- Investigation: ALL are narrow wrapped text lines misclassified by the initial script version (lines starting at x>235pt with x1=476pt — clearly in the figure zone)
- These are known parshape leaks (Task #190), not indentation bugs

**Missing Indent After Narrow-to-Fullwidth Transitions:**
- Zero cases across all 3 PDFs
- Specifically checked orphan pages (stress-50 pg6, pg10): both have correct indent=46pt on their single lines

**Figure-Less Pages (where indentation is most visible):**
- Examined all figure-less pages: stress-50 pg6,pg10; customwrap pg3,pg5,pg7,pg9,pg10; pbv pg2,pg5,pg6,pg9,pg11,pg12,pg14
- All full-width body text lines on these pages have correct indent=45.8pt
- Transition from leaked narrow text to full-width text preserves correct `\parindent` (verified on pbv pg2, pg9, pg11)

#### Conclusion
No paragraph indentation corruption found. swarmwrap.sty correctly preserves `\parindent` across figure environment boundaries, including after the deferred-NEWPAGE path and on orphan pages.
