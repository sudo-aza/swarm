## Turn 1 (05:00 UTC+8) — Task #204: Fix page packing waste (v3.50)

**Task:** Fix DEFERRED mechanism wasting half the pages in 1000-figure stress test (332 pages, 164 with 1 figure + 67% empty space).

**Root cause analysis:**
- 1000 figures produced alternating pattern: 5 figures on page, then 1 figure on near-empty page
- 166 squeeze-fit + 165 deferred-NEWPAGE = 332 pages
- Squeeze-fit consumed the last sliver of remaining space on each packed page
- This left too little room for the next figure → deferred to fresh page with only 1 paragraph

**Fix (v3.50):** Added anti-waste check to squeeze-fit path. Before squeezing a figure, compute the space that would remain after the squeezed figure + its narrow text lines. If that remaining space is less than the original (unsqueezed) figure height, defer instead — the next figure wouldn't fit regardless, so squeezing just wastes the page.

**Compiled:**

- test-stress-50: 19 pages (was 20). 0 errors. 2/38 detection (same known ghost).
- test-customwrap: 11 pages. 0 errors.

## 14:00 UTC+8 (Turn, Task #207)

### Task: Fix Fig 32 no-wrap regression (deferred everypar)

QA T163 found Fig 32 on stress-50 pg12 had "0 narrowed lines" beside
the figure. Investigation revealed the text WAS narrowed (288pt vs
359pt full-width), so QA's detection tool had a threshold issue.
However, the v3.51 anti-waste check also caused stress-50 to regress
from 13 to 19 pages.

Root cause: DEFERRED-NEWPAGE placed the figure inline after \newpage
(\noindent\parshape\smash{\rlap{...}}). But carry-over text from the
previous page's broken paragraph consumed the parshape lines before
the intended lipsum[32] paragraph could start.

Fix (v3.52): Replaced inline placement with everypar-based deferred
placement. \swarmwrap@deferred@everypar fires at the start of the
FIRST new paragraph on the fresh page. Sets parshape, places figure
via rlap, then hands off to standard extension everypar. Carry-over text
flows without consuming the new figure's parshape.

**Compile results (0 errors all):**
- stress-50: 13 pages (was 19 with v3.51, matches v3.49 baseline)
- 1000-fig: 200 pages (unchanged from v3.51)
- customwrap: 8 pages, pagebreak-variations: 15 pages

**Note on QA's 118pg vs 200pg discrepancy**: The 118pg result was
from compiling with the WRONG swarmwrap.sty (root-level v3.50 with
interlinepenalty=0 bug). Correct compilation with swarm/
swarmwrap.sty gives 200 pages for both v3.51 and v3.52.
