### Step 4.5: New Bug — Carry-Over Narrowing (Task #199)

During visual analysis requested by zoe (pages 23-24), discovered a distinct bug NOT captured by the existing detection metrics:

**Symptom**: On 7/46 pages, body text is narrowed at the TOP of the page before any figure starts. The parshape from the previous page's figure leaks across the page break.

**Affected pages** (PyMuPDF span-width analysis):
| Page | Narrow lines before figure | Wasted area | % of usable area |
|------|---------------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 9    | 6 lines                   | 19,362 sq pt | 9.0%            |
| 10   | 8 lines                   | 23,640 sq pt | 11.0%           |
| 24   | 6 lines                   | 12,593 sq pt | 5.9%            |
| 25   | 3 lines                   | 7,120 sq pt  | 3.3%            |
| 36   | 9 lines                   | 25,472 sq pt | 11.9%           |
| 41   | 6 lines                   | 14,942 sq pt | 7.0%            |
| 45   | 4 lines                   | 11,580 sq pt | 5.4%            |

**Example (page 24)**: Text at y=125-166 is narrowed to 260pt (right margin 99pt), but no figure exists there — Figure 27 starts at y=237. The narrowing is a carry-over from page 23's Figure 25.

**Distinction from existing bugs**:
- NOT ghost-narrowing (figure exists on same page, just not at the top)
- NOT the "32 excessive narrowing" count (aggregation artifact of page-level metric)
- NOT near-empty (pages have full content, just with unnecessary narrow columns)

**Root cause**: When TeX breaks a page mid-paragraph, the parshape set by swarmwrap.sty's Lua callbacks persists into the new page's paragraph start, even though the figure that justified the narrowing is on the previous page.
